This is the first movie in the Lord of the Rings Trilogy. It was released in 2001.

Synopsis

In the peaceful hills of the Shire, a young hobbit, Frodo Baggins fatefully comes across a magical ring of power in the aftermath of his uncle Bilbo Baggins's Birthday. Frodo discovers that Bilbo's ring is the One Ring forged by the Dark Lord Sauron. If returned to its master, the world of Middle Earth would crumble into ruin. He takes the role as the ringbearer to travel to Mount Doom, accompanied by his Fellowship, to destroy the ring once and for all.

Key Events (Spoiler Alert)

  • Frodo inherits the ring from his uncle Bilbo after Bilbo's 111th birthday party. Gandalf the Grey tells Frodo the ring's evil backstory
  • Frodo is accompanied by Sam, Merry, and Pippin to meet with Gandalf in the villiage of Bree. They meet Aragorn but are chased by Nazgul (ring wraiths in the process)
  • Frodo is mortally wounded and taken to Rivindell. A council is formed by Lord Elrond, the elven leader of Rivindell, to discuss the ring's fate
  • Frodo offers to take the ring to Mordor and destroy it. The Fellowship of the Ring is formed to accompany him on his journey
  • The Fellowship face many obstacles from Saruman, who as turned traitor for Sauron. They venture into the Mines of Moria, a dwarven city inside of a mountain. They encounter a Balrog, and Gandalf goes down with it into the depths.
  • The Fellowship meets the Lady of Light, Galadriel, in Lothlorien to find safe passage to Amon Hen.
  • An ambush at Amon Hen leads to the death of Boromir and the Fellowship breaks.
  • Frodo and Sam set out on the journey to Mordor while Aragorn, Legolas, and Gimli pursue the Uruk-hai horde that captured Merry and Pippin in the ambush.
